## Remark

For the proof for $\mathbb{K} = \mathbb{C}$, it suffices that
$$
\exists\, b \in \mathbb{R}^n \ \exists\, x^* \in \mathbb{C}^n \ \forall x_0 \in \mathbb{C}^n,
\quad
x_{k+1} := \Phi(b,x_k) : \quad x^* = \lim_{k\to\infty} x_k,
$$
i.e.,
$$
\text{"}\exists\, b \in \mathbb{C}^n\text{"}
\quad \text{instead of} \quad
\text{"}\forall\ b \in \mathbb{C}^n \text{"}.
$$
