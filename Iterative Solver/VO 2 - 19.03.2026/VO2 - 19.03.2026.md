# Splitting Methods
We aim to solve $Ax^\ast = b$ for given $A \in \mathbb{K}^{n \times n}$ regular, $b \in \mathbb{K}^n$ with sought $x^\ast \in \mathbb{K}^n$.
To this end, we consider the affine iteration function
$$

\Phi : \mathbb{K}^n \times \mathbb{K}^n \to \mathbb{K}^n,

\qquad

\Phi(b,x) := \Pi x + Nb

$$
with fixed matrices $\Pi, N \in \mathbb{K}^{n \times n}$. The matrix $\Pi$ is called **iteration matrix**.
**KNOWN:** We require $\rho(\Pi) < 1$ to guarantee convergence of the linear fixed point iteration

$x_{k+1} := \Phi(b; x_k)$ for arbitrary initial guesses $x_0 \in \mathbb{K}^n$.
